Apo Intanap

The Great Grandmother of the Togana Clan

Here's a time line of Apo Intanap's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cambulo, Ifugao
  • 1930 - An only child living with her parents at Cambulo, Ifugao. Helping her parents to work on the rice fields at a young age.
  • 1932 - Left the province to escape tribal wars during that time. Stayed at the border of Ifugao and Benguet for 5 months. Untill her and some people who also left Ifugao reached Benguet specifically at a place known now as "Tuba" near which is now Baguio City.
  • 1934 - Met the love of her life (She never did mention his name). They work also work at a small farm near from where they (migrated from Ifugao) settled in. Had three children during that time (names are unkown).
  • 1941 - The most scariest time of her life because of the invasion of Japan during world war 2. Apo Intanap and her children managed to survived the war and occupation. Unfortunately The love of her life and the father of their children might have died during the invasion, as he was not seen after the bombing and the occupation of the City.
  • 1944 - Apo Intanap and her children endured so much during the war. She had her fourth child (who is my grandfather) during the war. Speculated that she had her child with a japanese soldier during occupation.
  • 1947 - After the war Apo Intanap and her children thrive and managed to get back to their life.
  • 2000 - Apo Intanap was already 86 years old this time. All three of her children already passed. Only my grandfather was the only child left. She was under the care of her grandchildren.
  • 2012 - Apo Intanap developed dementia and near blindness of both of her eyes at the age of 98. Because of her dementia she always mistaken her granchildren to be her children and always gets scared that keeps talking about hiding because "the soldiers are coming" she says.
  • 2015 - Her last child (which is my grandfather) passed away, without her knowing due to her dementia and old age, at this time she barely can walk and see.
  • 2018 - Passed away at the age of 104, burried beside of all her four children.

"Apo Intanap's Life shows how she fought life with integrity and strength, Despite all the struggles she endured she proved that it can be managed. Also shows the love of her children that until the end they were all together"

-- Great Grandson Kharon Kade Togana
